The Dragon 2 is a class of partially reusable spacecraft manufactured by SpaceX, primarily used for flights to and from the ISS in addition to some private missions. It is a successor of the Dragon 1, and was developed along with the Boeing Starliner to replace the crew/cargo functions of the Space Shuttle. It first flew in 2019, and has flown a total of 11 crew and 8 cargo missions in space (as of March 2023)
