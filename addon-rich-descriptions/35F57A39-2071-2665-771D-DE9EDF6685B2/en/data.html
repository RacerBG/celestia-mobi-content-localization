Stardust is a NASA spacecraft which conducted the first sample-return mission to comets. Launched in 1999, it visited the asteroid 5535 Annefrank before taking a sample of the comet 81P/Wild's coma in 2004, which it returned to Earth in 2006. In 2011, it was then redirected to comet 9P/Tempel to investigate the crater left by Deep Impact in 2005, of which the latter hasn't been able to do so. A month after the Tempel 1 flyby, Stardust was decommissioned
