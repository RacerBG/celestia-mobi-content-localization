GJ 4332, also known as L 168-9, is a red dwarf star about 82.09 light-years away in the constellation Tucana. A superterran planet about 4.6 times more massive than Earth was discovered in a 1.4-day orbit around its star. Both objects will receive formal names at the end of the third NameExoWorlds campaign, and will be one of the first exoplanet systems that the JWST will observe and study
